3 The Indian Penal Code (IPC), 1860

Purpose

The Indian Penal Code is the substantive criminal law of India. It defines what constitutes a crime and specifies punishments for each offence. Drafted by Lord Macaulay, it came into force in 1862 and continues to be the most important criminal statute in India.

Structure

23 Chapters

511 Sections

Divided into parts dealing with:

  • General explanations and definitions (Sections 1–52).
  • General exceptions (Sections 76–106)
  • Offences against the State, public tranquility, human body, property, etc.

Key Provisions

Section 299–304: Culpable homicide and murder

Section 375–376: Definition and punishment of rape

Section 378–382: Theft and robbery

Section 415–420: Cheating and fraud

Section 499–500:Defamation

Importance

Applies to all citizens within India (except certain exceptions like the Army Act).

Provides a uniform criminal code across the country.

Forms the basis for FIRs, investigations, and criminal trials.
