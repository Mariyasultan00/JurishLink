3 The Indian Evidence Act, 1872

Purpose

This Act lays down rules of evidence — what facts and documents are admissible in a court of law, and how evidence should be evaluated.

Structure

3 Parts and 167 Sections

  • General explanations and definitions (Sections 1–52).
  • General exceptions (Sections 76–106)
  • Offences against the State, public tranquility, human body, property, etc.

Key Provisions

Section 3: Definition of “Evidence”

Section 24:Confession obtained by inducement or threat is invalid

Section 65B:Admissibility of electronic records

Section 101:“He who asserts must prove.” (Burden of proof)

Section 114:Presumptions the court may draw from facts

Importance

Prevents false or fabricated evidence.

Ensures that judgments are based on reliable and relevant facts.

Forms the core of the Indian judicial process.
