3 TThe Constitution of India, 1950

Purpose

The Constitution is the supreme law of India. Every law, rule, or regulation derives its authority from it. It defines India’s political, legal, and social framework.

Structure

4Preamble

25 Parts, 12 Schedules, and ~470 Articles

Key Provisions

Part III: Fundamental Rights (Articles 12–35)

Part IV:Directive Principles of State Policy (Articles 36–51)

Part IVA:Fundamental Duties

Part V & VI:Union and State Governments

Article 368:Amendment procedure

Importance

Protects citizens’ freedoms and equality.

Lays the foundation for democracy, secularism, and justice.

Acts as the touchstone for judicial review — any law violating it can be struck down.
