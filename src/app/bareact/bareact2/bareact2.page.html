3 The Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), 1973

Purpose

The CrPC is the procedural law that tells us how a criminal case should progress — from the filing of a complaint to the final judgment.

Structure

484 Sections + 2 Schedules

Divided into 37 Chapters

Key Provisions

Section 154: Registration of an FIR

Section 156:Power of police to investigate

Section 167:Procedure for remand (police/judicial custody)

Section 190–204:Taking cognizance and issue of process by Magistrate

Section 320:Compounding of offences

Section 357:Compensation to victims

Importance

Ensures fair trial and due process.

Balances powers between police, prosecution, and courts.

Protects the rights of the accused while ensuring justice for victims.
