3 The Code of Civil Procedure (CPC), 1908

Purpose

The CPC governs civil proceedings — disputes related to property, contracts, debts, etc. It ensures that all civil cases are handled systematically and fairly.

Structure

158 Sections and 51 Orders (Schedules)

Divided into:

  • Preliminary provisions
  • Suits, pleadings, trial, execution of decrees
  • Appeals, reference, review, and revision

Key Provisions

Order I–VII: Rules on how to file suits and pleadings

Section 9:Civil courts can try all suits unless barred

Order XXI:Execution of decrees

Section 96:Appeal from original decree

Section 115:Revision power of High Courts

Importance

Ensures uniform civil justice across India.

Protects citizens’ civil rights (property, contracts, damages, etc.).

Defines the procedure every civil court must follow.
