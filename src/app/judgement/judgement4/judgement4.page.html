Background

Rohit Sharma, a tech entrepreneur, challenged the admissibility of data collected from his private cloud account by police without a judicial warrant. He argued violation of his Right to Privacy as upheld in Puttaswamy v. Union of India (2017).

Issues

  • Can digital evidence obtained without a warrant be admitted in court?
  • Does the right to privacy extend to data stored on private cloud servers?

Judgment

The Bombay High Court ruled that unauthorized access to personal digital data without a court order constitutes violation of privacy under Article 21. Evidence obtained through such means is inadmissible in criminal proceedings.

Significance

The judgment established key procedural safeguards for law enforcement in handling digital data, setting a precedent for privacy protection in the digital era.
