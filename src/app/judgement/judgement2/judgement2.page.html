Rajesh Kumar v. State of Uttar Pradesh (2023)

Background

Rajesh Kumar, a journalist, was detained under the National Security Act (NSA) for publishing reports critical of district administration practices. He petitioned under Article 226 claiming misuse of preventive detention provisions.

Issues Considered

  • Whether preventive detention can be invoked for non-violent expression.
  • Whether the Detaining Authority must provide detailed reasons accessible to the detenue.

Judgment

The Allahabad High Court quashed the detention order, stating that invoking preventive detention against journalistic expression undermines democratic freedoms. The Court directed that all preventive detention orders must be reviewed within 10 days by a judicial officer.

Impact

The decision established stronger judicial oversight in preventive detention matters, marking an important step toward protecting freedom of speech and press accountability.
