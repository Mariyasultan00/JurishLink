State of Delhi v. Rahul Singh (2025)

Delhi High Court • Criminal Law – Unlawful Detention

Background

Rahul Singh was accused of participating in a public protest that turned violent. He was detained by the Delhi Police without a warrant and was held for 72 hours before being presented before a magistrate. His counsel argued that this was a violation of Article 22(2) of the Constitution.

Issues Raised

  • Whether detention without a warrant violates Article 22(2) and the Code of Criminal Procedure.
  • Whether the protest activity constituted a cognizable offense warranting immediate arrest.

Judgment

The Delhi High Court ruled that the police failed to justify the extended detention without producing Rahul Singh before a magistrate within the constitutional timeframe. The Court ordered disciplinary proceedings against the officers involved and awarded compensation to the petitioner.

Significance

This judgment reaffirmed the inviolability of the right to personal liberty and due process, reiterating that even preventive actions must comply strictly with constitutional safeguards.
